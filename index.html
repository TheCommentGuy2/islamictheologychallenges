Islamic Theology Challenges

Extensive Rigorous logical examinations of Islamic theological arguments and doctrines

These are systematic logical analyses of specific arguments used to justify Islamic doctrines and defend traditional Islamic theology.

Each challenge presents a logical tree where you choose how to defend the position. Every path is explored. Every response is tested. The logic reveals the consequences.

These arguments have been refined through extensive debate with Muslim apologists and draw on both Islamic theology and contemporary philosophy of religion.

The Euthyphro Dilemma
Is something good because God commands it, or does God command it because it is good? This dilemma challenges whether objective morality can be grounded in God.
Targets: Divine Command Theory, Moral Arguments for God
The Sincere Rejection Problem
Can billions of non-Muslims really be "arrogantly rejecting truth they know"? Human psychology makes this impossible. This challenges doctrines of eternal punishment for honest disbelief.
Targets: Islamic Hell doctrine, "Arrogant rejection" theology
The Interfaith Miracles Dilemma
Why do prayers "work" in all religions? If Allah answers prayers directed to idols, He validates shirk—the worst sin. This creates an impossible theological problem.
Targets: Islamic exclusivism, Miracle claims as evidence

These challenges are offered in pursuit of intellectual honesty and philosophical rigor.
